How is Ayn Rand's philosophy relevant today?

Any Rand's philosophy: "My philosophy, in essence, is the concept of man as a heroic being, with his own happiness as the moral purpose of his life, with productive achievement as his noblest activity, and reason as his only absolute." Rand's philosophy is relevant today because it is lands on a society where people may feel more concerned about their own self-interest and this idea of only "looking out for yourself". If this is done properly and practiced by everyone, people who follow Rand believe the world could be a better place. Rand's ideas are truly the philosophy of objectivism.
This philosophy is relevant today because of its problematic nature in teaching people to in effect become egotistical. The issue with objectivism is the lengths people will go to achieve their own happiness. Rand advises that people be ethical about their decisions towards their own happiness, but a person's own interpretation of what qualifies an action as ethical is up to them. What one person thinks is ethical, another may disagree with. This issue is, if we live a life where we do only what leads to our happiness, this causes relationships to fail. A tenet of a relationships is sometimes putting others before yourself meaning their happiness over yours. As a Catholic, this philosophy completely contradicts the foundation of my religious beliefs that ask the church to give what they can and volunteer time to help the need and afflicted. I believe that people attempting to follow this philosophy will find themselves unhappy and unfulfilled.
